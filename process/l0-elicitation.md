# L0 elicitation protocol

The finite set of questions asked of a product's founder to produce its Preamble (L0).
This is the operational substance of Article **F-V**, Step A of
[defining-l0-l1.md](defining-l0-l1.md).

## Why it's finite

A Preamble needs its claims to be **identity-level, durable, distinct, and minimal**. So
the protocol is exactly **one extraction pass + three filters + one apex**. More questions
re-extract the same identity; fewer drop a required axis.

```
extract (Q1–3)  →  bound (Q4–5)  →  filter (Q6–7)  →  apex (Q8)
candidate claims    sharpen to        keep only the      seeds the top of
                    identity edges    durable, minimal   the priority order
```

## The questions

**Extract — surface the raw claims**
1. **Founding insight** — What did you come to believe about your domain that the existing
   tools get wrong? (why you, why now)
2. **Core unit** — What is the one noun your product organizes everything around — the noun
   that, if swapped, changes the product? (the sharpest identity contrast)
3. **Distinctive outcome** — What does a successful user gain — and *why does that matter
   to them*? (the outcome and the purpose behind it)

**Bound — find the edges of the identity**
4. **The non-negotiable** — Finish: "We would rather lose a user than ___."
5. **Negative space** — Name something competitors do that you will deliberately refuse to
   build. Why?

**Filter — is this really L0?**
6. **Durability** — Five years out, the stack, UI, and feature list have all changed. What
   single sentence about the product is still true?
7. **Subtraction** — For each candidate statement: if you deleted it, would it still be the
   same product? (this is what forces L0 down to ≤3)

**Apex — bridge to conflict resolution**
8. **The tie-breaker** — When two values you hold collide, which wins **by default**? If
   you believe they aren't truly opposed (one *produces* the other), name which is the
   means and which is the end — and still name the fallback when forced to choose.

## Reading the answers

Two signals worth watching for (both observed on the founding DSAMind run):

- **Convergence is bedrock.** When extraction (Q2) and the durability filter (Q6)
  independently land on the *same* answer, that claim is almost certainly L0 — you found it
  twice by different roads. DSAMind hit this: Q2 and Q6 both returned "pattern fluency."
- **An apex answer that names two values is unfinished.** Founders often answer Q8 with a
  pair ("rigor and speed"). Push for an order: ask which is the *means* and which is the
  *end*, and which wins when forced. DSAMind resolved to "rigor produces speed → rigor wins
  by default" — a both/and that still yields a default.

## Composing the answers into L0

- Q1–3 yield candidate statements.
- Q4–5 sharpen each candidate into an identity claim.
- Q6–7 filter to the durable, minimal few.
- Distill to ≤3 statements; the human ratifies (F-V — the agent phrases, never originates).
- Q8 also seeds the apex of the product's priority hierarchy (conflict-resolution.md).
