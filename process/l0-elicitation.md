# L0 elicitation protocol

The finite set of questions asked of a product's founders to produce its Preamble (L0).
The operational substance of Article **F-V**, Step A of [defining-l0-l1.md](defining-l0-l1.md).

## What a Preamble must establish

A constitution governs **parties**, and a complete Preamble covers four dimensions:

```
parties  →  who the constitution governs (commonly the owner + its users)

Solution · Value   →  the MISSION  (what the product is + the value it creates for users)
Grow · Monetize    →  the MANDATE  (how the platform grows + gets paid for that value)
```

- **Mission** (Solution + Value) → the user-facing P-lines.
- **Mandate** (Grow + Monetize) → the owner-facing P-line(s).

A Preamble that names the Mission but not the Mandate is half a constitution — it forgets the
business must survive. (DSAMind hit exactly this: its first run produced P1–P3 and no owner
mandate; P4 was added only after the founder flagged "we make money" — which is why the Grow
and Monetize questions now exist.)

## Why it's finite

Name the parties, cover the four dimensions, filter for durability + minimality, set the
apex. More questions re-extract; fewer drop a dimension.

## The questions

### Parties — who this governs
1. **Parties** — Who are the parties this constitution governs? Commonly the **owner** and its
   **users**; some products have more (creators, buyers, sellers, moderators). Every Article
   is later tagged with the party it protects.

### Solution — what you solve / what you are
2. **Founding insight** — What did you come to believe about your domain that the existing
   tools get wrong?
3. **Core unit** — What is the one noun your product organizes everything around — the noun
   that, if swapped, changes the product?

### Value — the value created for users
4. **Distinctive outcome** — What does a successful user gain, and why does that matter to them?
5. **The non-negotiable** — Finish: "We would rather lose a user than ___."
6. **Negative space** — Name something competitors do that you will deliberately refuse to
   build. Why?

### Grow — how the user base grows
7. **Acquisition + retention** — How does a stranger become an active user, and what makes an
   active user bring others? What do you give away free to earn the next user?

### Monetize — how the value is paid for
8. **The paid boundary** — What does a user pay for, and what stays free forever? The value
   you charge for vs. the value you give away to grow.

### Filters — is each claim really L0?
9. **Durability** — Five years out, the stack, UI, and feature list have all changed. What
   single sentence about the product is still true?
10. **Subtraction** — For each candidate statement: if you deleted it, would it still be the
    same product (and the same business)? Forces ≤3 Mission lines + ≤1 Mandate line.

### Apex — bridge to conflict resolution
11. **The tie-breaker** — When two values you hold collide, which wins by default? If they
    aren't truly opposed (one produces the other), name the means, the end, and the fallback.

## Reading the answers

- **Convergence is bedrock.** When the core unit (Q3) and durability (Q9) land on the same
  answer, that claim is almost certainly L0. (DSAMind: both = "pattern fluency".)
- **An apex answer that names two values is unfinished.** Push for an order. (DSAMind:
  "rigor produces speed → rigor wins by default".)
- **Grow and Monetize answers become the Mandate**, not the Mission — and the *durable
  principle*, not the current tactic. "Free loop, paid coach" is L0; this quarter's price or
  trial length is L2 policy.

## Composing the answers into L0

- The parties (Q1) define the `party:` vocabulary for every Article.
- Solution + Value (Q2–6) → the **Mission**: ≤3 user-facing statements.
- Grow + Monetize (Q7–8) → the **Mandate**: the owner-facing line(s).
- Q9–10 filter to the durable, minimal few; the human ratifies (F-V — agent phrases, never
  originates).
- Q11 seeds the apex of the priority hierarchy (conflict-resolution.md).

## Status

`RATIFIED` (with F-V) — exercised on DSAMind (2026-06-28): produced its Mission (P1–P3) and,
after the run surfaced the gap, its Mandate (P4). The run fed back the **parties +
Solution/Value/Grow/Monetize + Mission/Mandate** frame (plus earlier Q-sharpening on the
distinctive outcome and the apex order).
